A new field,
unfamiliar faces as far as I can see,
All they can see are the differences.
Un matching wool, a foreign name,
a accent that echoes somewhere else.
Yet expecting me to fall into line,
to be herded quietly into the flock.
Ive only heard stories of joy,
stitched between each blade of green,
the roots that he established,
that feel so foreign to me.
But in this field, as strange as it is,
It is some forgotten part of me,
My father grew in this soil,
ran through this grass,
his laughter sewn into its roots,
He belonged here.
